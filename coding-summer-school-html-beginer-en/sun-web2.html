A wonderful story

From the dawn of his history, man looked up to heaven for indications of future events, and he diligently followed the heavenly commands, as these brought into his life the necessary order, giving him a sense of security and stability. No wonder, then, that he also located the abode of the gods, whose deeds and achievements were expressed in countless legends and myths passed down from generation to generation like the long and wide world. Edwin C. Krupp gives an extensive overview of these tales of heaven, successfully trying to demonstrate their fundamental unity, resulting from the universal nature of man's relationship with the cosmos, and at the same time the remarkable diversity resulting from cultural diversity. Thanks to the popularizing talent of the author, the book is easy to read, although the fascinating stories contain quite a lot of solid knowledge. Reading about ancient ideas about the sun, moon and other celestial bodies, we imperceptibly realize that we are discovering the roots of our own culture, in which much of what was once the content and backbone of civilization is still preserved. The feeling of delight that seizes a man at the sight of heaven and that elusive border that separates us from it, and the desire that in fact makes us human, ordering us to move forward to explore what is there - for the horizon. Dr. Edwin C. Krupp - astronomer and historian of astronomy, director of the Griffith Observatory in Los Angeles, author of, among others Books: Sky watchers, shamans and kings ("Na pathy Naukach" series, 2001).
